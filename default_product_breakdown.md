## Product Overview

A short summary of what the product does, who it's for, how it makes money, and what stage the company is in. Use this to get oriented before diving into the rest of the analysis.

## Competitive Landscape

Who the main competitors are, how this product differs from them, and where it sits in the market (leader, challenger, or niche). Helps you understand defensibility and positioning.

## Product Strengths (What's Working)

Three or more things the product does well, with concrete examples. Use this to see what's already working and why, so you can reference it in interviews or when suggesting improvements.

## Areas for Improvement

Three or more weaknesses or gaps, with why they matter. This section highlights opportunities and tradeoffs the team is likely already thinking about.

## Key Metrics to Track

A table of metrics that matter for this product (e.g. engagement, retention, revenue) and why each one is important. Useful for aligning on success and for interview discussions about measurement.

## Experiment Ideas

Concrete experiment ideas: what you'd test, the hypothesis, and how you'd measure success. Shows you can move from "what's wrong" to "how we could learn and improve."

## Interview Prep: Questions to Ask

Smart, product-minded questions you could ask if you were interviewing at this company. Covers strategy, growth, tradeoffs, and direction so you sound thoughtful and prepared.
